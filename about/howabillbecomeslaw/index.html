How a Bill Becomes Law

This page explains, in detail, every major procedural step and decision point a bill may encounter in the Michigan Legislature.

1

Introduction

A bill may be introduced in either chamber of the Legislature.

  • Senate bills are filed with the Secretary of the Senate.
  • House bills are filed with the Clerk of the House.
  • Each bill is assigned a number upon introduction.

Bill Numbering

  • House Bills begin at House Bill 4001 each biennium.
  • Senate Bills begin at Senate Bill 1.
  • Joint resolutions are assigned letters.

Title Reading & Printing

  • The Michigan Constitution requires three readings.
  • This requirement is satisfied by reading the bill’s title.
  • The bill must be printed and in possession of both houses for at least five days.
2

Committee Referral & Action

Upon introduction, the bill is referred to a standing committee.

Committee Review

  • Members debate and analyze the bill.
  • Public hearings may be held.

Committee Action Options

  • Report the bill favorably.
  • Report the bill favorably with amendments.
  • Report a substitute bill.
  • Report the bill without recommendation.
  • Report the bill with amendments but without recommendation.
  • Recommend referral to another committee.
  • Take no action.
  • Vote to not report the bill.

Failure to Report

  • A motion to discharge the committee may be offered.
  • Requires a majority of members elected and serving.
  • In the House, one-day prior notice is required.
3

Second Reading / General Orders

  • Committee recommendations are considered.
  • Members may offer amendments.
  • Majority vote advances the bill.
4

Third Reading & Floor Vote

  • Final debate occurs.
  • Roll call vote is taken.
  • Bill may pass, fail, or be delayed.

Delay Options

  • Returned to committee.
  • Postponed indefinitely.
  • Postponed to a date certain.
  • Tabled.
5

Second Chamber & Conference Committee

  • Bill follows the same process in the second chamber.
  • If amended, both chambers must agree.
  • A conference committee may be appointed.
  • Conference reports may not be amended.
6

Governor’s Action

  • Governor signs the bill.
  • Governor vetoes the bill.
  • No action taken (bill may become law).

Veto Override

  • Requires two-thirds vote in both chambers.
7

Effective Date

  • Most laws take effect 90 days after session adjournment.
  • Immediate effect requires a two-thirds vote in both chambers.
