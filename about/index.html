About the Michigan Legislature

The Michigan Legislature is the lawmaking branch of state government. It is a bicameral body composed of the Senate and the House of Representatives, meeting at the State Capitol in Lansing.

Overview

The Legislature enacts laws, adopts resolutions, and maintains official legislative records for the State of Michigan. Through debate, committee review, and recorded votes, elected members represent the people in shaping public policy.

Legislative Structure

The Senate consists of 38 members serving four-year terms. The House of Representatives consists of 110 members serving two-year terms. Together, both chambers must approve legislation before it may be presented to the Governor.
